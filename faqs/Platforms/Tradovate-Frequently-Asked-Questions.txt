Tradovate Frequently Asked Questions

Overview
This guide explains common Tradovate issues, troubleshooting steps, and frequently asked questions related to Apex Trader Funding Tradovate accounts.


Live Market Data Issues

Why Is My Data Not Live on Tradovate?

To ensure live market data works correctly, follow these steps.

Step 1 — Log In
Use the Tradovate login credentials provided with your Apex account.

Step 2 — Sign Required Agreements
Before live data can activate, you must sign the required documents including:

Non-Professional Market Data Agreement

Other required data agreements

These agreements must be completed before live market data can activate.

Step 3 — Wait for Data Activation
After signing the agreements, live market data activation can take:

10 to 90 minutes

This timing depends on platform demand.

Do not submit a help desk ticket until at least 90 minutes have passed.

Step 4 — Verify Your Instrument
Make sure the correct instrument contract is loaded in Tradovate.

Incorrect or expired contracts may appear to have no live data.

Step 5 — Check Data Status
If the instrument appears in orange, the data is delayed and not live.

Important:
Do not trade with delayed data. Trading without live market data may lead to significant losses.

Monthly Data Fees
If the Non-Professional agreement is not signed, market data fees may exceed:

$115 per month



Common Tradovate Errors and Solutions


Error: Failed to Login — TradingView Not Entitled to Access Your Account

Cause
Your TradingView account is not properly linked with Tradovate.

Solution
Log in to Tradovate and complete the TradingView connection setup using the official Tradovate integration instructions.



TradingView Charging $9.99 for an Add-On

Cause
The free TradingView subscription requires monthly renewal.

Solution
Renew your TradingView connection every 30 days.

Check the subscription status and renewal date within TradingView.



TradingView Connection Stops Working Monthly

Cause
TradingView subscriptions require monthly reactivation.

Solution
Reactivate the TradingView connection on the same calendar day each month as the original setup date.



No Live Data on the First of the Month

Cause
Market data may not automatically reactivate on the first day of the month.

Solution
If live data has not activated after 6 AM ET, submit a Help Desk ticket.



Roll Forward Message — Contract Expiration

Cause
You are attempting to trade a futures contract that is nearing expiration.

Solution
Find the correct front-month contract using CME Group market tools.

Roll your contracts forward before expiration.



Error: Liquidation Only — Contract Expired

Cause
You attempted to trade an expired futures contract.

Solution

Research the correct front-month contract.

Roll the contract before expiration.

Trading platforms like NinjaTrader and Tradovate will typically show contract roll warnings.



Expired Positions That Cannot Be Closed

Cause
Positions in expired contracts cannot be closed normally.

Solution
Tradovate automatically expires contracts at:

9:30 AM ET on expiration day.

If positions remain open, they may stay until market close.

Always monitor expiration dates carefully.



Preventing Tradovate Problems

To reduce technical issues:

Stay updated on Tradovate platform changes.

Monitor contract expiration dates.

Roll contracts forward early.

Renew TradingView subscriptions monthly if using the integration.

Account Transfers Between Platforms

Can I Convert a Performance Account Between Tradovate and Rithmic?

No.

Accounts cannot be transferred between platforms.

Account history, balances, and trading days cannot move between Tradovate and Rithmic accounts.

To switch platforms, you must pass a new evaluation and activate a new Performance Account.



Order Rejections

Why Are My Orders Being Rejected?

Most commonly this occurs because the order size exceeds your account’s contract limit.

Verify that the number of contracts entered does not exceed the maximum allowed for your account size.

Blown Accounts

Why Is My Account Missing After It Was Blown?

Blown Tradovate accounts are automatically removed at the end of the trading day.

Once removed:

The account disappears from the platform.

Trade history for that account is no longer accessible.
