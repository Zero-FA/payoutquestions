EOD Drawdown Explained

Overview
The End-of-Day (EOD) Drawdown defines the maximum total drawdown allowed on an account.

Key characteristics:

- The EOD Drawdown is calculated once per trading day at market close.
- It is enforced in real time during the following trading session.
- If the threshold is reached, all open positions are automatically liquidated.
- Reaching the threshold results in evaluation failure or Performance Account (PA) closure.
- The EOD threshold never moves downward.


What is the EOD Drawdown?
The End-of-Day (EOD) Drawdown sets the lowest balance your account is allowed to reach.

It is calculated once per day at market close based on the account’s End-of-Day balance.

Once calculated, the EOD Drawdown becomes active during the next trading session.

If the account balance touches or falls below the EOD Drawdown during a trading session:

- All open positions are automatically liquidated.
- In an Evaluation account, the evaluation fails.
- In a Performance Account (PA), the account is closed.


How the EOD Threshold is Calculated
The EOD Threshold is recalculated once per trading day at 4:59:59 PM ET.

It is based on the account’s closing End-of-Day balance.

Important rules:

- The threshold trails the highest End-of-Day balance achieved.
- The threshold never moves downward.
- Each account starts with a fixed drawdown amount based on account size.


Intraday Enforcement
Even though the EOD Drawdown is calculated at market close, it is enforced in real time during the next trading session.

If the account balance touches or falls below the threshold during the session:

- All positions are automatically liquidated.
- Trading stops immediately.
- The evaluation fails or the Performance Account closes.


When the EOD Drawdown Stops Trailing

Performance Accounts (PA)
For Performance Accounts, the EOD Threshold stops increasing once it reaches:

Starting Balance + $100

Example for a 50K EOD Performance Account:

Threshold Stop Level = $50,100

This occurs when the highest End-of-Day balance reaches:

$52,100 (Starting Balance + Maximum Drawdown + $100)

From that point forward, the threshold remains fixed at $50,100.


Evaluations (Rithmic and WealthCharts)
For Rithmic and WealthCharts Evaluation accounts, the EOD Threshold stops trailing once it reaches the target profit level.

Example for a 50K Evaluation account:

Threshold Stop Level = $53,000

This occurs when the highest End-of-Day balance closes above:

$55,000 ($53,000 + $2,000)

After that point, the threshold remains fixed at $53,000.


Evaluations (Tradovate)
For Tradovate Evaluation accounts, the EOD Drawdown continues trailing indefinitely based on the highest End-of-Day account balance.


Frequently Asked Questions

Do I fail if I reach the EOD Threshold intraday?
Yes. If your account balance touches or falls below the EOD Threshold at any time during the trading session:

- All open positions are automatically liquidated.
- Your evaluation fails or your Performance Account is closed.

Even though the threshold is calculated at end-of-day, it is enforced in real time.


Does the EOD Threshold move?
Yes, but only upward.

The threshold increases when your End-of-Day balance reaches a new high. It never moves downward after losing trading days.


What happens to the threshold on losing days?
If the account has a losing day, the EOD Threshold does not move. It remains at the previous level even if the account balance is lower.


What is the difference between the Daily Loss Limit (DLL) and the EOD Threshold?
The Daily Loss Limit (DLL) limits how much an account can lose within a single trading day.

If the DLL is reached:

- All open positions are liquidated.
- Trading stops for the remainder of the day.
- The account remains active and trading resumes the next session.

The EOD Threshold defines the maximum total drawdown allowed across the entire life of the account.

If the EOD Threshold is reached:

- Positions are liquidated immediately.
- The evaluation fails or the Performance Account is closed permanently.

In simple terms:
The DLL ends your trading day.
The EOD Threshold disqualifies your evaluation or closes your account.


Can my account dip below the threshold intraday if it later recovers?
No.

The account may experience intraday drawdown, but it may never touch or cross the EOD Threshold level at any time.


What is the difference between the Live Trailing Threshold and the EOD Threshold?
Both thresholds protect the account from exceeding the maximum drawdown, but they are calculated differently.

Live Trailing Threshold
- Recalculates continuously in real time.
- Trails the highest intraday account balance.
- Can move upward during the trading session.

End-of-Day (EOD) Threshold
- Calculated once per day at market close.
- Based on the End-of-Day account balance.
- Remains fixed during the following trading session but is enforced in real time.

In simple terms:
The Live Trailing Threshold moves with intraday performance.
The EOD Threshold is calculated at market close and enforced during the next trading day.
